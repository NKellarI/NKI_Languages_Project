General History:

Most of the key ideas of Lisp were developed from the summer of 1956 to the summer of 1958, which were originally implemented in the FORTAN-based FLPL, and in the fall of 1958 to 1962, they were applied to problems of artificial intelligence. Lisp was developed at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). Though Lisp would go through many iterations and branches, such as Clojurecs, Emacs Lisp, and Common Lisp, Julia, Racket, and Scheme, these branches or varying implementations are known as dialects.

Uses:

Lisp was founded on the mathematical theory of recursive functions, or functions that call themselves. A Lisp program is a function applied to data rather than something like Python, where a sequence of procedural steps takes place. Lisp exploded the AI scene at the time,partly because AI programs capable of learning could be written by Lisp as self-modifying programs. Its capabilities were numerous, as it has pergull macros, the language is itself programmeable, and it operates on most platforms.

Current State:
